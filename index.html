THE MOON

The moon changes its shape in the sky, and we call these changes phases. The moon doesn't produce its own light, but reflects light from the sun. When the moon is between the sun and Earth, we can't see it, and this is called the New Moon. As the moon moves in its orbit, a small part of it becomes visible, and we see a Crescent Moon. The moon continues to move, and more of it becomes visible, forming a Half Moon. Eventually, we see the entire lit side of the moon, and that's called the Full Moon. After the Full Moon, the moon starts to shrink again, and we see less of it until it becomes a Half Moon once more. Finally, only a small sliver of the moon is visible, and it becomes a Crescent Moon again.
